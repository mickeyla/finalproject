After analyzing your time, you should develop better habits to make better use of your time!

Click on the tabs to recieve tips on your school, sleep, social, and work.

School Tips
A change of scenery changes the way you study. Move from the usual desk space to coffee shops, bookstores, libraries, etc and allow your brain to retain information better.
Track your work. Keeping a dedicated planner helps you better organize the tasks you to have.
Look over your notes each night to make sure you have the material down pact. If you want to take a further step, re-writing notes always helps retain information.
Learn to accept failure! You can't always be perfect and therefore, you are bound to fail. Just learn from your mistakes and be better.
Effectively make use of a class. Make friends for help on homework, actively make yourself known by your teacher, attend tutoring sessions, finish classwork on time. Using classtime effectively minimizes the work you have to do at home.
Sleep Tips
Develop a schedule to stick to. Do your night routine and get in bed at a specific time each night.
Avoid naps! Tire yourself out through the day and sleep well at night!
Create a comfortable sleeping enviornment. If you like the area in which you sleep and are comfortable sleeping, you'll have a better time of it.
Avoid your phone and any bright lights that might keep you awake. Read a book, pick up journaling.
If it gets really bad, don't be afraid to talk to your doctor.
Social Tips
Initiate conversation with new people!
Keep eye contact, smile, and listen. People like to know that you are engaged in their side of the conversation.
Make your mark! Make a good first impression to maintain good long-lasting friendships.
Ask people you are conversing with questions about themselves and their background to show that you would like to know more about them.
Keep in contact! Get numbers, names, emails. Try to keep up a consistent connection.
Work Tips
Keep a positive attitude! Even when projects are tough, keep going and push onward!
Be on time. Being late shows that you no longer care about your job.
Get to know your colleagues but don't be there to gossip. You are there to work, not invest time in petty gossip.
Be a professional; accept constructive criticism. Feedback is meant to help you grow and learn, not take personal offense.
Go the extra mile to show that you are passionate in your job. Do this by avoiding distractions and setting goals for each and every day!
Work hard, but don't over do it! You might work hard, but working hard doesn't necessarily mean working exhausting hours. Be able to balance your social and work life.

“Until we can manage time, we can manage nothing else.” - Peter Drucker.
