Bio

I graduated from Rose L. Hardy Middle School and went on to attend McKinley Technology High School for grades 9 and 10. I am currently a junior at Cesar Chavez Parkside High School. In school, I participate in Future Business Leaders of America (FBLA), FIRST Robotics: Team 1915, Army JROTC and Drill Team, and Certified in some Microsoft Office Applications.

Every summer I work in the Summer Youth Employment Program. I was one of about 30 applicants who were accepted to work at the United States Holocaust Memorial Museum as a raising 2018 Holocaust Ambassador. I completed my 14 weeks and graduated from the Bringing the Lessons Home program and I am a Holocaust Museum Ambassador for life. I also worked at the Georgetown Law School Site for Girls Who Code 2018. There, I learned more complex programming languages and worked on networking with my fellow workmates and the guest speakers.

I am 16 and the oldest of all of my siblings. I have eight sisters and one brother. I was born in Washington D.C. and I have resided here and in South Carolina.

I like sketching and art, performing and singing, robotics, coding, writing poetry, and playing my instruments. My favorite food is Pork n’ Chive Dumplings and my least favorite food is chicken.

My Poetry

Betral Knows My Name

Betrayal knows my name. I know only a one way game. Made of different paths. All going down the wrong road. Passageways to what is the unknown.

Love and hate are both strong words, Two of which are never applied. To the cruel reality of the world. Fairy Tales, called Tales for a reason. No one lives without a conflict.

Moon, Sun, Rain, Clouds, go away. Stay away, never come back until I say. Welcome to a world of pain. Where only god knows the truth. Hence why Betrayal Knows My Name.

Broken

I layed on my bed, with a broken heart, I kept thinking, and my heart did the same by beating, on a fatal and unforgiven night, I stood with my heart beating,

the only boy I like layed in a street, only moments before his body was thrown in a creak, alone I would find myself across the road, I saw everything, as my only friend passed, his soul

moves on, then I stand alone, as a man drew near, wrecked, my soul wanted to fly away with him, as the man held up his bloody plank you only live once and my hope broke,

mine was soon to be over, too soon if I may add, as he got closer he moved faster, all in the night of Christmas Eve, the boy was happy, to end up in the heavens above, we were not meant to end in a scary night, I’ve prayed to die happy in my sleep, and in the bell

of time I slowly closed my eyes, dropped to the ground and sat down, my time wasn’t up and I knew that well, if I was closer down

to the ground i’d be closer to the boy, who hit the bad guy to save me again, a bad guy indeed, why are they in the world, I was an idiot to take sides with the man, an idiot to believe I could be with the boy too, I never would have chosen him if I had known, but I knew none.
